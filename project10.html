Case Closed

Designing a mobile-first detective experience that makes investigation intuitive through evidence collection, suspect comparison, and logical deduction.

📄 View Case Study

The Challenge

Making investigation feel intuitive

Existing detective games often rely on cluttered interfaces and guesswork, making investigations frustrating instead of rewarding. The goal was to design a mobile-first experience that supports logical reasoning through intuitive interactions.

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

Early concepts & structure

Early layouts explored how players would navigate between suspects, evidence, and crime scenes while keeping the experience simple for short play sessions.

Finished Product

The finished interface

Usability Testing

Testing with real users

We conducted usability testing to evaluate how intuitively users could navigate the investigation experience. Participants were asked to complete key tasks like finding clues, switching tabs, and identifying suspects.

10 Participants
Usability ★★★★☆ 4.6
Navigation ★★★★★ 4.7
Clarity ★★★★☆ 4.3

Key Findings

Players successfully navigated the interface, discovered evidence, and completed investigation tasks with high confidence. Feedback highlighted the suspect comparison feature as particularly effective while suggesting improvements such as autosave and larger text for smaller devices.

Reflection

What I took away

Designing Case Closed taught me that successful interaction design is about supporting how users think. By combining user research, iterative prototyping, and usability testing, I learned how thoughtful interface design can transform a complex investigation into an intuitive and engaging experience.
